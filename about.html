Current Situation on Mental Health
Mental health and psychoactive substance-related disorders are prevalent globally, affecting people of all backgrounds, ages, races, and socio-economic statuses.
Approximately one in eight individuals, or nearly one billion people, live with a mental disorder, making these conditions the leading cause of years lived with disability (YLDs). Severe mental health conditions, such as schizophrenia, contribute to significantly reduced life expectancy, with individuals often dying 10 to 20 years earlier due to preventable physical diseases.
A large portion of those with mental health conditions remain untreated, with only 29% of people with psychosis receiving mental health services globally. Depression and anxiety are the most common mental health disorders, affecting 13.5% of women and 12.5% of men worldwide, with women being more prone to these conditions. In contrast, men are more vulnerable to substance use disorders.
Suicide is a leading cause of death, especially among adolescents and young people, and is a major global concern. In Tanzania, which has one of the highest suicide rates in Africa, suicide disproportionately affects individuals aged 15–29 years. Mental health issues such as anxiety, depression, and substance abuse are significant contributing factors. Stigma, the criminalization of suicide, and the absence of suicide prevention helplines exacerbate the issue, discouraging people from seeking help.
